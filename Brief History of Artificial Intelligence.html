Brief History of Artificial Intelligence

A Karel Capek play entitled "Rossum's Universal Robots (RUR)" which was premiered in London used the word 'robot' for the first time in London -1923.
Neutral Network Base Work-1943.
Isaac Asimov, a Columbia University researcher, creat the term "robotics"-1945
T'Alan Turing" introduction of 'Turing test' for intelligence research. A groundbreaking paper entitled "Computing Machinery and Intelligence" was also written -1950.
John McCarthy was the originator of the word "artificial intelligence." At Carnegie Mellon University a prototype of the AI system was attempted -1956.
John McCarthy's Innovation in LISP programming language for AI -1956.
For the first time at the MIT, Joseph Weizenbaum launched ELIZA, an immersive software that translates messages into English -1965.
Edinburgh University's Assembly Robotics group developed the invention of popular Scottish robot 'Freddy' which is efficient enough to locate, collect, and build models using vision -1973.
Stanford Cart, the creator of the first computer-controlled autonomous vehicle-1979.
Harold Cohen created and built a drawing system called 'Aaron' -1985.
